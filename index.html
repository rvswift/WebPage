ABOUT DR. SWIFT

Scientific Adventures

  • 1978

    Beginnings

    I was born in San Jose, CA and lived there until I was one, when my parents relocated to Santa Barbara CA, where I was raised.

  • 1998 - 2003

    Santa Barbara City College

    As a student at Santa Barbara City college, I fell into the sciences and teaching. There, I tutored general and organic chemistry for the Chemistry Department and algebra, precalculus and calculus for the Math Department. I graduated with honors and earned AA degrees in liberal studies, chemistry, math and an AS degree in physics.

  • 2003 - 2005

    UC Santa Barbara

    I transferred to UC Santa Barbara as a chemical engineering major but changed to biochemistry after realizing how much I enjoyed the blend of organic chemistry and biology it offers. For two years, I worked for Campus Learning Assistance Services tutoring general and organic chemistry, which solidified my interest in teaching. I graduated with highest honors and a BS in biochemistry.

  • 2005 - 2009

    UC San Diego

    My graduate research at UC San Diego was multidisciplinary, and I performed physics-based computer simulations of enzymes to study the relationship between enzyme motion and function. I was also a teaching assistant for a variety of lower and upper division chemistry courses and earned an MS and PhD, both in chemistry.

  • 2009 - 2016

    Postdoc and Beyond

    I did postdoctoral research at UC Irvine in the Pharmaceutical Sciences Department. There, I developed computer models to enable drug discovery and design. After two years, I returned to UC San Diego as a project scientist. There I continued to research and teach for the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry, and I was a research programer for the Drug Design Data Resource. I also spent a year consulting in the Scientific Computing Department at Dart Neuroscience.

  • 2016 - 2017

    Cal State University San Marcos

    I want to make a positive impact on the next generation of STEM students, so I returned to school to earn a teaching credential and begin a career as a high school teacher. Backed by generous scholarships from the National Science Foundation and the American Chemical Society, I'm currently completing a secondary teaching credential in chemistry.
